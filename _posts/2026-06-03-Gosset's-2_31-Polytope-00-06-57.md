<a href="">Nan Ma</a> produced vZome models for orthographic <i>zomeable</i> projections of Gosset polytopes. His H3-symmetric projection (with 30 hull vertices) of the 2_31 polytope looked very appealing. The 2_31 only has <b>93 distinct 3D ball positions</b> after projection. In the Zometool model, these will be represented by <b>orange</b> connectors. Due to the projection from 7D to 3D, a lot of edges either everlap or intersect. As bending Zometool struts is a very bad idea, all these false overlaps and intersections must be dealt with. Therefore, in the vZome model for the building instructions, all overlapping struts have been removed, and most (840) of the 1080 pairs of crossing struts have been replaced by <b>false vertices</b>, each connecting at least four Zometool struts. These false vertices are represented by <b>white</b> connectors:

3D printing is used to handle the remaining pairs of crossing struts. In step 3, <a href="https://joris1724.github.io/vzome-sharing/2026/06/03/00-06-57-Gosset's-2_31-Polytope/y1.scad">y1 struts with a hole</a> were designed and 3D printed. These allow a regular b1 to pass through in step 4, instead of connecting y00, y0, b00 and b0 struts. The y00 and b00 struts whould have to be 3D printed and are difficult to handle.

These allow a regular b3 to pass through in step 14, instead of having to connect two hb2's and two hb3's. Both have been once produced by Zometool, but the hb3's are rather rare.
