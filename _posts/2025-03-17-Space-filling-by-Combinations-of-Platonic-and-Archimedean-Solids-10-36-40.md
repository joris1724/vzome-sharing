In his book <i>Structure in Nature is a Stategy for Design</i> Peter Pearce enlisted 23 space filling systems requiring multiple different types of polyhedra. Of these 23, only eleven (systems 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 16, 17, 18, 19 , 20 and 23) require no multiple prism packings and therefore do not give rise to infinite numbers of space filling permutations. Only four of these (systems 5, 6, 8 and 20) have co√∂rdinates in the golden fiel, and can b build with Zometool green struts only. I modeled the other seven with vZome using (38&phi;-53)/12 as an approximation for &radic;2, which is off by less than 1/800000. These can be build exactly with Zometool, if one is prepared to use both blue and blue-green struts.
