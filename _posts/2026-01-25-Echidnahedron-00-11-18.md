Building a Zometool model of the Final Stellation of the Icosahedron would require to put two green Zometool struts in the same pentagonal hole, as shown in the upper half of the picture:

<figure style="width: 87%; margin: 5%">
       <img src="https://joris1724.github.io/vzome-sharing/2026/01/25/00-11-18-Echidnahedron/Echidnahedron.png" >
  <figcaption style="text-align: center; font-style: italic;">
    Redirecting g2 struts from pentagonal to rectangular holes
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This impossibility can be bypassed by designing (see <a href="https://joris1724.github.io/vzome-sharing/2026/01/25/00-11-18-Echidnahedron/Echidnahedron-g2.vZome">vZome<a> and <a href="https://joris1724.github.io/vzome-sharing/2026/01/25/00-11-18-Echidnahedron/Echidnahedron-g2.scad">Openscad<a> files) a modified g2 strut, as shown in the lower half of the picture: instead of bending towards a pentagonal hole, it bends in the other direction towards the neighbouring rectangular hole, at both ends of the strut. The tips of the struts are generated by vZome's <i>3D Printing Starters</i>.
<p>
